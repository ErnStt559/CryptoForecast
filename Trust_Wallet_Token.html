Trust Wallet Token (TWT) Price Forecast 2025: In-Depth Analysis

The information in this article is provided for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice. Cryptocurrency investments carry high risks.

Introduction

Trust Wallet Token (TWT) is the native utility token of Trust Wallet, a popular mobile cryptocurrency wallet launched by Binance in 2020. TWT offers holders in-app benefits, such as discounts on purchases and decentralized exchange (DEX) trades, as well as governance rights within the Trust Wallet ecosystem. As of August 2025, TWT trades at approximately $0.77, with a circulating supply of ~416.65 million tokens and a market capitalization of ~$320 million. This is significantly below its all-time high of $2.72 in December 2022. Trust Wallet supports over 100 blockchains and 10 million+ tokens, boasting over 200 million downloads globally, making it a cornerstone of the crypto wallet space. This analysis explores TWT’s price outlook for 2025, focusing on market trends, growth drivers, risks, competition, adoption, and volatility.

Current Market Context

In 2025, TWT has underperformed, declining ~12% year-over-year and trading ~70% below its 2022 peak of $2.72. As of late August 2025, it hovers around $0.77 with a 24-hour trading volume of ~$8.57–$14.37 million, representing only ~3.5% of its market cap, indicating low liquidity. Technical indicators are mixed: the 50-day moving average is rising, suggesting short-term bullishness, but the 200-day moving average has been declining since July 2025, signaling longer-term weakness. Volatility over the past 30 days is moderate at ~3.1–5.39%, with 43–57% “green” days. The Relative Strength Index (RSI) ranges between 43–51, indicating neutral momentum. TWT faces resistance at ~$0.80–$0.84 and support at ~$0.70–$0.75, with low trading volumes contributing to price stagnation.

Risks and Downside Factors

  • Market Volatility: TWT’s thin trading volume (~3.5% of market cap) makes it susceptible to sharp swings, especially in a 30–50% crypto market correction.
    • [](
  • Regulatory Concerns: Stricter KYC/AML regulations or restrictions on non-custodial wallets could reduce Trust Wallet’s user base, impacting TWT demand.
  • Competition: Rivals like MetaMask and Coinbase Wallet could erode Trust Wallet’s market share if they offer superior features or integrations.
  • Stalled Adoption: If Trust Wallet’s growth slows or new features (e.g., FlexGas) fail to gain traction, TWT’s utility and price could stagnate.
  • Binance Dependency: TWT’s ties to Binance make it vulnerable to negative developments affecting BSC or Binance, such as regulatory actions or reduced collateral ratios.

Volatility and Technical Outlook

TWT’s 30-day volatility is moderate at ~3.1–5.39%, with 43–57% “green” days. The RSI (43–51) is neutral, and the price trades near the 50-day SMA ($0.76) but below the 200-day SMA ($0.86), indicating short-term stability but longer-term weakness. Resistance levels at $0.80–$0.84 have capped recent rallies, while support lies at $0.70–$0.75. A breakout above $0.84 could target $1.25–$1.50 in a bullish market, but failure to hold $0.75 may lead to a drop toward $0.60–$0.70. TWT’s price is heavily influenced by broader crypto sentiment and liquidity constraints.

Conclusion

TWT’s 2025 price is likely to range between $0.70–$1.10, with a base case of ~$0.90. Bullish scenarios (up to $1.50) depend on a crypto market rally and successful feature adoption (e.g., FlexGas). However, risks like volatility, regulatory pressures, and competition from other wallets could keep TWT subdued. Investors should monitor Trust Wallet’s user growth, BSC developments, and market trends while conducting thorough research due to TWT’s high volatility and market dependency.
