Quant (QNT) Price Forecast for End of 2025: Comprehensive Analysis and Insights

The information in this article is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice. Cryptocurrency investments carry significant risks.

Introduction

Quant (QNT), launched in June 2018 by Gilbert Verdian, is a pioneering blockchain interoperability platform. Its flagship product, Overledger, is the world’s first blockchain operating system, enabling seamless communication between disparate blockchains and legacy systems. The QNT token, an ERC-20 token on Ethereum, is used for licensing Overledger services and paying transaction fees, driving demand through enterprise adoption. As of August 27, 2025, QNT trades around $107–$110, with a market cap of approximately $1.3 billion, ranking #66–70 on CoinMarketCap. This article analyzes QNT’s price outlook for the end of 2025, exploring recent trends, growth drivers, and risks.

Current Situation

In 2025, QNT has shown moderate price movement, rising from the mid-$90s in early 2025 to ~$107 by late August, with a market cap of ~$1.3 billion. Trading volumes remain modest, with 24-hour volumes around $23.7 million. Technical indicators suggest consolidation, with support at $99–$100 and resistance at $108–$111. The 50-day SMA (~$104.7) and RSI (~45) indicate a neutral-to-bullish trend, with low volatility favoring long-term holders. Recent gains reflect growing enterprise interest, notably Quant’s role in CBDC projects like the Bank of England’s Project Rosalind and the ECB’s Digital Euro prototype.

  • Blockchain Interoperability: Overledger’s API-based platform connects blockchains like Ethereum, Polygon, and Avalanche, enabling multi-chain applications (mApps) and seamless data transfer, critical for DeFi and tokenized assets.

Volatility Analysis

From July to August 2025, QNT fluctuated between $106 and $115, with low volatility compared to other altcoins. Support lies at $99–$100, with resistance at $108–$111 (200-day EMA). The 50-day SMA ($104.7) and RSI (~45) suggest neutral momentum, with potential for an upward break if Bitcoin’s rally continues. A Fibonacci resistance at $127.45 poses a challenge; breaking it could target $144, while failure may retest $94. Quant’s enterprise focus and fixed supply reduce volatility, but a market correction could test lower supports.

Conclusion

By late 2025, QNT is likely to trade between $100 and $150, with potential to reach $200–$250 in a bullish scenario, driven by Overledger’s interoperability, CBDC partnerships, and a possible crypto rally.
